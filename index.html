    • Good posture is essential for both physical and mental health. It involves aligning the spine, shoulders, and pelvis to reduce strain on muscles and joints, preventing pain and discomfort. Historically, in ancient Greece, posture was seen as a symbol of moral virtue and strength. Proper posture improves lung capacity, digestion, and circulation by allowing organs to function optimally. It also boosts confidence, focus, and energy, leading to better productivity and mood. In the modern world, where prolonged sitting is common, being mindful of posture can prevent injury and reduce fatigue. Simple habits like sitting upright, standing tall, and taking breaks can promote long-term health. Developing good posture is a key to well-being.
